Prompt Guide

How to write a great prompt

01
Set the stage
Tell AI who you are and what you're working with. Your property type, location, guest profile, and any relevant context. The more specific your situation, the more relevant the answer.
02
Define the task
Be clear about what you want AI to do. Write, analyse, summarise, or build, and specify the format. A subject line and three-paragraph email is more useful than "write me an email."
03
Set the rules
Add any constraints on tone, length, audience, or things to avoid. If you have an example of what good looks like, include it. Rules turn a generic output into one that fits your brand.
Example prompt
Win-back email for a lapsed guest
01 Set the stage
I manage a 14-room boutique hotel in the Yarra Valley, Victoria. We focus on weekend escapes for couples aged 30–55. Our tone is warm and personal.
02 Define the task
Write a re-engagement email for guests who haven't returned in 12+ months. Include a subject line, preview text under 90 characters, a short email body, and a single call-to-action with a deadline.
03 Set the rules
Keep it under 120 words. Don't use the words "exclusive" or "deal." The offer is 15% off for stays booked before 30 June. Make it feel personal, not like a mass campaign.
